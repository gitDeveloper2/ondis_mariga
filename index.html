1

Analysis and Estimate

We normally go ahead to advise that clients take a similarly cross-disciplinary approach at this point, and bring members from marketing, legal or any department that will want to have a say later in the project. If it’s an internal web-app project, we emphasise the importance of bringing along someone who’s actually going to be managing or, on the ground using the web app. This makes sure that all these stakeholders have a voice at the outset, rather than once the process is underway.

2

Design

It’s a little more fluid than other stages, because an input from one of the later steps might require going back to the drawing board to implement it. But generally speaking, we go through five steps, in this order: wireframe, concepts, collaborative design, prototyping, and user testing.

3

Develop

Every page on the website or webapp is laid out in a way that’s easy to follow and gives visitors a sense of continuity. This is supported by things like supporting videos or images in the same location, a standard text format, and much more.

4

Product launch!

Having completed each of the stages and our client is satisfied, the website/ webapp sitaken live and, target audience has started using it. At this point, we are left to provide support.
